## Group work on my project
Time goes so fast and this semester is going to end soon. I feel like I gained a lot without even noticing it. This week, I am still working on an issue to fix a bug on the web. This bug was mentioned by three users in the issue discussion, and it is about shortcut keys. Compared to the beginning, I feel more calm and confident when facing the process and the codebase.

During this process, I really felt how amazing the open source community is. Even though our repo only has one active maintainer right now, he is very active on Discord and responds pretty quickly. Some older contributors are also always there to help and answer questions from newcomers, just like me one month ago. Their kindness made me feel really warm and safe.

This also made me think more about the idea of open source. It is not just about writing code, but also about helping others, sharing knowledge, and building something together. I realized that even small contributions or answering simple questions can make a difference in the community.

<!--more-->

What's more, our group started preparing for the final presentation. All my group members are very responsible and efficient. We almost finished our slides during the in-class meeting and completed them before our regular online meeting on Thursday. This really helped us practice our presentation smoothly. I feel very lucky to work with such a responsible and friendly group.

Another thing I really appreciate is that my group members and I all agreed that we will continue contributing after the course ends. Whether it is continuing with AFFiNE or trying new projects, we all want to keep being part of open source.

Looking back, I think choosing a beginner-friendly open source project was a very good decision. It allowed me to slowly build confidence and actually contribute. I also learned that starting is always the hardest part. Once you begin, things become much clearer step by step.

At the end, I still want to say: don’t think too much, just go for it XD
